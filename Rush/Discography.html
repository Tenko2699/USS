DISCOGRAPHY

Rush

"Rush" is Rush's debut studio album, launched on March 18, 1974, in Canada by Moon Records and internationally by Mercury Records that same year. Recorded 5 years after the band's formation, the album showcases a distinctive hard rock sound reminiscent of popular rock bands from the early 1970s. Influenced by bands like Led Zeppelin, Yes, and Cream, their musical inspiration is evident throughout most of the album's tracks.

ALBUM

Fly by Night

"Fly by Night," Rush's second studio album, released on February 14, 1975, via Mercury Records, marked the band's venture into progressive rock, solidifying their signature style. This album also introduced Neil Peart as the drummer and primary lyricist, succeeding John Rutsey the previous summer before the band's first North American tour. 

Album

Caress of Steel

"Caress of Steel," Rush's third studio album, released on September 24, 1975, via Mercury Records, marked a transition from their blues-based hard rock debut to a more progressive rock sound. However, this shift led to commercial and critical challenges, with the album's darker tone and complex compositions initially failing to resonate with audiences. Poor sales even threatened the band's relationship with Mercury Records. Although later appreciated by fans, it remains an unconventional entry in Rush's catalog, often referred to as "the black sheep" by critics.

Album

2112

2112 (pronounced "twenty-one twelve") is the fourth studio album by Canadian rock band Rush, released in March 1976 (or April 1, 1976, according to some sources) by Anthem Records. 2112 was released to favourable reviews from music critics and quickly outsold the band's previous albums. It peaked at No. 5 on the Canadian Albums Chart and No. 61 on the US Billboard Top LPs & Tape and was the band's commercial breakthrough in the country.
